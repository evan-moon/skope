# The Herald Family

You're reading the docs for **skope**, the news layer of a small family of local-first tools. They share one principle: **your data stays on your machine, and the AI comes to it** — no uploads, no cloud, no account.

## Three tools, one stack

| Tool | What it is | What it owns |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **skope** — *you are here* | news intelligence | a personalized lens on the world — the news that reaches *you* |
| **firma** | financial intelligence | portfolio · net worth · cash flow, in `~/.firma/firma.db` |
| **memex** | persistent memory | your notes and the context behind them, across every session |

Each is an independent MCP server you install on its own. None depends on the others — but Claude can hold all three at once, and that's where they compound: skope can read your firma holdings to score which news actually reaches you, and memex remembers why you started following a story skope first surfaced.

This is exactly skope's [Federation](/en/docs/federation) principle, seen from above: skope owns its interest profile, and firma or memex are *optional adapters* Claude reads from — never dependencies skope calls.

## Federation, not lock-in

The tools never call each other. Each owns its own data and exposes its own MCP tools; Claude is the only thing that sees across them. Add or drop any one and the rest keep working. That's the whole design — **compose through the model, not through a backend.**
